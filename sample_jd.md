# Job Description: AI Engineer Intern

## Company

upGrad

## Position

Internship

## Job Location

Mumbai

## Qualification

B.Tech, M.Tech, or MCA, pursuing or recently graduated.

## Duration

3-6 months

## Stipend

30,000 per month

## Role Overview

upGrad is building AI-native learning experiences for millions of working professionals globally. As an AI Engineering Intern, you will work directly on LLM-powered products, from prototyping intelligent solutions to improving overall learning experiences.

This is a hands-on builder role for someone who learns fast, ships faster, and is genuinely curious about what AI can do for education.

## Job Description

### AI/LLM Development

- Build and experiment with LLM-powered features, including RAG pipelines, AI agents, and prompt engineering workflows, for upGrad's learning and content products.
- Develop and test prototypes for personalisation, content generation, and learner engagement use cases.
- Assist in evaluating model outputs for quality, relevance, and safety in an education context.

### Collaboration And Integration

- Work closely with product managers, learning scientists, and senior engineers to translate learner problems into AI solution briefs.
- Integrate AI modules into existing product workflows using APIs and internal tooling.
- Document experiments, findings, and implementation decisions for cross-functional teams.

### Research And Iteration

- Stay current with developments in LLMs, agentic frameworks, and AI in education, and propose experiments worth running.
- Debug, test, and iterate model performance based on user feedback and product metrics.

## Skills Required

- Strong Python programming skills, with clean and readable code, not just working code.
- Hands-on familiarity with LLMs, LangChain, RAG pipelines, or AI agents through projects, coursework, or personal experimentation.
- Basic understanding of model evaluation and deployment concepts.
- Ability to work independently, ask the right questions, and communicate clearly with non-technical stakeholders.
- A builder's mindset, defaulting to trying something before waiting for a complete specification.
